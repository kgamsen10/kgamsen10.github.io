The first ever caution sign was made in 1908 at the Automobile Association in West London. There isn't one person credited with creating the picture, but after WW2, OSHA was developed and they forced the making of the caution we know today, There goal was to make a picture obvious to tell you to not walk there without blocking the walk way and overall improve workplace safety.
